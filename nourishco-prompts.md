# Toddle Case Study Prompt

## NourishCo: Supplier Onboarding & Compliance Platform

## About This Assignment

Product managers are expected to drive products end-to-end - from understanding user and business problems to defining strategy, prioritizing effectively, collaborating across teams, and driving successful execution and outcomes. This take-home assignment is designed to evaluate how you:

- Break down ambiguous business problems
- Define product strategy and scope
- Prioritize effectively under constraints
- Balance user needs with business goals
- Think through workflows, systems, and scalability
- Leverage AI meaningfully within product experiences and operations

AI use is encouraged and expected. We want to understand how you think with AI as a collaborator, not whether you avoided it.

## Business Challenge

NourishCo is a global FMCG enterprise that owns multiple brands across food, beverages, personal care, and sustainable packaging:

- PureSip Beverages - Premium bottled water & flavored drinks.
- Harvest Bites - Snacks & healthy food (granola bars, organic chips).
- DailyGlow - Personal care (skincare, haircare, beauty).
- QuickEats - Frozen & ready-to-eat meals.
- EcoPack - Sustainable packaging solutions.

NourishCo collaborates with thousands of suppliers for raw materials and packaging. However, supplier onboarding and compliance tracking currently vary across brands, leading to:

- Delays in approvals due to scattered document management.
- Lack of real-time compliance visibility across brands.
- High manual effort in tracking certifications, audits, and contract renewals.
- Duplicate supplier approvals across brands, increasing inefficiencies.

Additionally:

- Procurement teams across brands follow slightly different review processes
- Supplier data quality is inconsistent
- Compliance requirements differ across categories and geographies
- Leadership lacks centralized visibility into supplier risk and operational bottlenecks.

## The Solution

NourishCo aims to develop a centralized, multi-brand supplier onboarding & compliance platform that:

- Simplifies supplier onboarding through a unified registration experience
- Enables suppliers to apply across multiple brands without duplicate onboarding
- Automates compliance tracking for certifications, audits, sustainability checks, and renewals
- Empowers procurement teams with configurable review and approval workflows
- Provides centralized visibility into supplier compliance and operational health
- Supports role-based permissions across suppliers, procurement teams, and central operations
- Leverages AI to reduce manual effort, improve operational efficiency, and surface risks proactively

## Your Product Management Challenge

Define the product strategy and execution approach for NourishCo's supplier onboarding & compliance platform. Your solution should demonstrate strong product thinking, prioritization, systems design, and execution planning.

You should think through:

- How suppliers onboard and manage compliance across multiple brands
- How procurement teams review, approve, and monitor suppliers
- How the platform prevents duplication and maintains a unified supplier identity
- How workflows differ across brands and regions
- How AI can improve efficiency, reduce operational overhead, and surface insights
- How the system handles operational edge cases, stress scenarios, and scale

You may make reasonable assumptions where required, but clearly state them.

## Deliverables

### Research & Insights

- Identify key stakeholders, user segments, and compliance pain points
- Conduct competitor or market analysis
- Extract key user and business insights
- Highlight assumptions, dependencies, and unknowns

### Product Requirements Document (PRD)

- Problem statement, goals, and non-goals
- Key workflows and user journeys
- Functional and non-functional requirements
- AI-assisted workflows and automations
- MVP scope, prioritization rationale, and success metrics
- Stress cases, edge cases, and scalability considerations

### Playable Prototype Using AI

Create a playable prototype using AI-powered prototyping tools that demonstrates:

- Product workflows
- Information architecture
- Navigation structure
- Systems thinking and core decision-making experiences

## Evaluation Criteria

- Product thinking: Ability to identify the core problems, define priorities, and make thoughtful tradeoff decisions.
- Strategic clarity: Clear articulation of product vision, MVP boundaries, prioritization rationale, and long-term scalability.
- Systems thinking: Ability to think across workflows, permissions, data consistency, operational complexity, and multi-brand scalability.
- Execution planning: Ability to translate ideas into actionable product requirements, phased rollout plans, and realistic implementation approaches.
- User and business understanding: Strong understanding of enterprise workflows, stakeholder needs, operational realities, and business impact.
- AI & automation thinking: Practical and innovative use of AI to improve workflows, reduce manual effort, surface insights, and enhance operational efficiency.
- Stress case & edge case handling: Ability to anticipate operational failures, scale challenges, exceptions, and real-world complexity.
